About

Independent research, engineering, art, and systems development.

0xdeadbeef is the public working identity of an independent researcher, engineer, artist, designer, developer, writer, photographer, musician, Bitcoin builder, cybersecurity practitioner, investigator, and founder working across technical, creative, scientific, and strategic fields.

Overview

One multidisciplinary practice.

The work presented throughout this website is organized as a single continuous practice rather than a collection of unrelated careers.

The practice spans software, web systems, firmware, hardware, mobile applications, embedded electronics, Bitcoin infrastructure, artificial intelligence, machine learning, cryptography, OSINT, cybersecurity, cyber investigations, geopolitical research, writing, visual art, design, photography, music, cannabis botany, field research, and business development.

Creative practice informs technical design. Engineering informs art. Security influences architecture. Bitcoin influences organizational and financial systems. Field research and international experience influence the geopolitical and cultural context in which projects are developed.

The objective is not merely to produce demonstrations. The objective is to create systems that can be inspected, operated, repaired, maintained, documented, extended, and preserved.

Projects range from public websites and open-source software to private research tools, embedded hardware, laboratory systems, data platforms, technical publications, visual media, and long-term institutional planning.

Professional Development

From creative practice to integrated engineering.

Foundations

Developed early interests in drawing, painting, design, photography, computing, electronics, history, science, philosophy, environmental study, and independent learning.

2006–2007

India

Lived in Mumbai while continuing academic and independent study. Traveled extensively across India, including Himalayan, northern, western, eastern, and southern regions, establishing a lasting interest in South Asian culture, politics, infrastructure, religion, and technological development.

2008–2010

Engineering Transition

Expanded into software, networking, Linux, cryptography, automation, embedded systems, renewable energy, digital music, web development, information security, and early Bitcoin infrastructure.

2010

San Francisco

Developed web systems, software, applications, embedded devices, firmware, hardware concepts, cryptographic workflows, Bitcoin nodes, payment systems, wallets, analytics, and automation.

2016–2020

AI, ML, OSINT, and Cybersecurity

Expanded into local AI, machine learning, computer vision, data systems, public-record research, geospatial analysis, digital investigations, malware analysis, security engineering, and cyber-conflict research.

2020–2026

Organizations and Integrated R&D

Began structuring long-term technology, research, consulting, publishing, Bitcoin, hardware, software, security, AI, and laboratory ventures within the developing ZZXL and ZZX-Labs organizational ecosystem.

Ventures

Organizations designed for long-term continuity.

Beginning in the early 2020s, long-term organizational planning expanded into a developing parent-subsidiary ecosystem involving ZZXL, ZZX-Labs, research companies, consulting entities, software ventures, hardware programs, Bitcoin infrastructure, publishing, creative work, and laboratory development.

The purpose of this structure is to preserve founder control, separate operational risks, maintain research independence, support distinct product lines, and create durable institutions around work that would otherwise remain fragmented across individual projects.

Prospective international planning includes research, consulting, logistics, laboratories, manufacturing, publishing, software operations, partnerships, and technical development across selected regions in North America, Europe, the Middle East, South Asia, East Asia, and Oceania.

Planned locations, companies, laboratories, subsidiaries, partnerships, and operational regions should not be interpreted as completed deployments until formally established.
