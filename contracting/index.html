Overview

Contracting for implementation, production, integration, and delivery.

Contracting differs from consulting. Consulting provides analysis and direction. Contracting provides defined implementation work, technical production, research execution, documentation, integration, or delivery.

Every contract requires a written scope, deliverables, schedule, payment structure, client responsibilities, acceptance criteria, ownership terms, and change-control process.

Projects may be completed independently or in coordination with internal teams, external developers, laboratories, legal counsel, designers, engineers, researchers, manufacturers, or institutional partners.

Work is accepted only where authority, access, legality, safety, technical feasibility, and required resources can be established.

Contract Process

From inquiry to delivery.

Project intake

The client submits objectives, requirements, technical context, expected deliverables, schedule, budget, jurisdiction, and authority.

Discovery and feasibility

The project is reviewed for completeness, technical feasibility, dependencies, risks, access requirements, and unknowns.

Statement of work

A written scope defines deliverables, exclusions, milestones, payment, ownership, licensing, confidentiality, acceptance, and change control.

Execution

Work proceeds according to the approved plan, with records, testing, version control, progress reporting, and issue tracking as required.

Review and acceptance

Deliverables are submitted for evaluation against the written acceptance criteria and approved revision process.

Handoff and closure

Final files, documentation, credentials, source, build instructions, licenses, warranties, and maintenance terms are transferred as agreed.

Client Responsibilities

Successful delivery requires timely client participation.

Clients must provide accurate requirements, lawful authorization, access, technical materials, decision-makers, test environments, credentials, source data, and timely feedback.

Delays in access, approvals, payment, information, third-party dependencies, or client review may alter the schedule and cost.

Clients are responsible for legal, regulatory, compliance, privacy, licensing, export, safety, operational, and institutional approvals applicable to their use of the deliverables.

Production deployment should occur only after appropriate testing, review, backup, security assessment, and operational preparation.

Ownership and Licensing

Rights are defined by the written agreement.

Preexisting Materials

Existing tools, libraries, frameworks, methods, templates, and intellectual property remain with their original owner unless explicitly transferred.

Project Deliverables

Ownership, assignment, licensing, reuse, publication, attribution, and source access are specified in the contract.

Open Source

Open-source components remain subject to their respective licenses and cannot be reassigned contrary to those terms.

Restricted Work

Some projects require additional authorization or cannot be accepted.

Authorization required

Security testing, investigation, controlled technologies, regulated systems, sensitive data, government work, defense-related work, critical infrastructure, surveillance-related work, and high-risk engineering require appropriate authority, legal review, access controls, and written limitations.
