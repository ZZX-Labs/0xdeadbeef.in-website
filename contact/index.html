Before Contacting

Provide enough information for a serious qualification review.

Professional inquiries should identify the sender, organization, project, jurisdiction, technical problem, expected outcome, timeline, funding status, intended users, relevant systems, and preferred engagement type.

Messages containing only vague requests, unexplained attachments, shortened links, unsolicited credentials, unverified claims, or demands for immediate access may not receive a response.

Security-sensitive inquiries should begin with minimal non-sensitive context. Do not include exploit code, private keys, passwords, access tokens, personal records, classified material, restricted technical information, or confidential evidence in the first message.

Use OpenPGP for sensitive correspondence after independently verifying the relevant fingerprint through more than one trusted channel.

No unsolicited secrets

Never send seed phrases, private keys, authentication credentials, passwords, API secrets, confidential datasets, restricted evidence, or sensitive personal information through ordinary email.

Security Contact

Responsible disclosure and sensitive technical reports.

Security reports should identify the affected domain, repository, application, device, version, environment, prerequisite access, reproduction conditions, impact, proposed severity, and whether the issue has been disclosed elsewhere.

Include only the minimum proof necessary to establish the issue. Redact personal data, credentials, private keys, tokens, customer records, and unrelated system information.

Do not perform destructive testing, persistence, data extraction, privilege expansion, service disruption, lateral movement, social engineering, or testing against third-party infrastructure without explicit written authorization.

Reports may be rejected when they concern self-XSS, scanner-only output, unsupported software, purely theoretical issues without impact, missing authorization, or systems outside the published scope.

Emergency limitations

This contact page is not an emergency service, law-enforcement dispatch channel, crisis hotline, managed security operations center, or guaranteed real-time incident-response service.

Response Policy

What to expect after sending an inquiry.

No guaranteed response

Publication of an email address does not guarantee acceptance, availability, review, representation, engagement, support, or a response.

Relevant inquiries receive priority

Clear, lawful, funded, technically relevant, well-scoped inquiries are easier to qualify than vague or speculative messages.

Unsolicited attachments may be ignored

Unexpected executables, archives, office documents, disk images, scripts, links, or encrypted files may be deleted without review.

Security reports require safe handling

Do not include unnecessary exploit material or sensitive records. Begin with a minimal description and wait for secure-channel instructions where appropriate.

Engagement begins only in writing

No consulting, contracting, representation, confidentiality, partnership, employment, or client relationship exists until expressly agreed in writing.

Identity may require verification

High-risk, high-value, sensitive, governmental, institutional, or security-related inquiries may require proof of identity, authority, and organizational affiliation.

Privacy and Records

Communicate only what is necessary.

Messages may be retained for security, legal, evidentiary, contractual, archival, or operational purposes. Do not send information that is unnecessary to evaluate the inquiry.

Encryption protects message content in transit and at rest only to the extent that endpoints, keys, software, metadata, and operational practices remain secure.

Email metadata may still reveal sender, recipient, timing, routing, subject, message size, and infrastructure details even when the body is encrypted.

Use separate communication channels for fingerprint verification and high-value instructions. Treat any unexpected request to change payment details, keys, domains, or identity as suspicious.
