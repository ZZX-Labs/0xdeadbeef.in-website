Overview

Independent work requires independent infrastructure.

0xdeadbeef and ZZX-Labs R&D develop independent technical, creative, scientific, and research systems without relying on conventional venture-capital ownership structures.

Funding helps sustain software development, hardware prototyping, embedded systems, Bitcoin infrastructure, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, OSINT, technical research, documentation, publishing, art, photography, music, and field work.

Funding Model

Direct support without equity dilution.

General donations do not purchase equity, ownership, governance rights, profit participation, debt claims, tokens, securities, intellectual-property rights, access rights, consulting time, contractual deliverables, or guaranteed recognition.

Unless expressly agreed in writing, a contribution is treated as voluntary support for independent work and may be allocated according to current technical, research, operational, and maintenance priorities.

Project-specific sponsorships, research grants, equipment gifts, contracts, consulting engagements, licensing arrangements, procurement, and institutional funding require a separate written agreement.

No donation creates a partnership, agency relationship, fiduciary duty, employment relationship, client relationship, confidentiality obligation, or obligation to complete a particular project.

Directed Funding

Supporting a particular project or research program.

A supporter may identify a preferred project, discipline, equipment need, publication, experiment, repository, prototype, or research area through the contact page.

Include the transaction ID, amount, approximate broadcast time, intended project, and whether public acknowledgment is requested or prohibited.

Directed funding is not binding unless accepted in writing. A project may be delayed, revised, merged, suspended, discontinued, or redirected when technical, legal, ethical, financial, security, or operational conditions require it.

Where a restriction cannot be accepted, the contributor may be informed before the contribution is treated as restricted funding, provided adequate contact information was supplied.

Finality and Refunds

Bitcoin payments are generally final.

No guaranteed reversal

Bitcoin transactions cannot be reversed by the website, a payment processor, a bank, or a card network. Verify the amount, network, address, and transaction fee before signing and broadcasting.

General voluntary support is ordinarily nonrefundable once confirmed on the Bitcoin network.

Sending funds to an incorrect address, wrong network, incompatible asset, fraudulent substitute address, or compromised clipboard may result in permanent loss.

Any exceptional return of funds is discretionary, may require identity and ownership verification, and may be reduced by network fees, legal costs, tax effects, compliance requirements, exchange-rate changes, or administrative expense.

No refund request should include a seed phrase, private key, password, or wallet backup.

Security

Protect against impersonation and payment substitution.

Use only official domains

The canonical portfolio domain is https://0xdeadbeef.in/. Do not trust lookalike domains, added subdomains, alternate spelling, or a prefixed www host.

Do not trust social-media messages

A direct message, comment, advertisement, video description, or profile post is not sufficient proof of a funding address.

Verify OpenPGP where appropriate

For substantial institutional or restricted funding, verify identity through the fingerprints and public keys published on the contact page.

Inspect wallet output

Confirm the destination, amount, fee, and network on the final signing device rather than relying only on the computer clipboard.

Reject urgent substitutions

Pressure to send immediately, bypass verification, use a new address, or disclose wallet information is a strong indicator of fraud.

Report suspected impersonation

Use the contact page to report fraudulent domains, altered addresses, impersonation, phishing, fake fundraising, or compromised publication channels.
