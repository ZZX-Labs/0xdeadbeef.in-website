Independent Practice

Technology, research, design, and creative systems.

This website is the central public archive for the work of 0xdeadbeef: an independent engineer, researcher, artist, designer, developer, writer, photographer, and systems builder.

The portfolio spans software, firmware, hardware, applications, Bitcoin, artificial intelligence, machine learning, open-source intelligence, cybersecurity, geopolitics, art, music, photography, writing, cannabis botany, and business development.

Projects are documented with source code, design notes, system architecture, visual studies, technical specifications, research papers, screenshots, field reports, and development histories where appropriate.

The long-term objective is to maintain a durable, searchable, privacy-respecting archive of original work across technical and creative disciplines.

Working Principles

Build clearly. Document thoroughly. Preserve knowledge.

Engineering should remain understandable after the original implementation context has disappeared. Systems should be explicit, portable, testable, and documented well enough to reproduce.

Open standards and local ownership are preferred over unnecessary dependency, opaque platforms, and centralized control.

Technical work exists within political, economic, ecological, and social systems. Research therefore requires attention not only to what can be built, but also to who controls it, who benefits, and how it may fail.

Bitcoin, cryptography, privacy, open-source software, and independent infrastructure are treated as practical tools for individual and institutional resilience.
