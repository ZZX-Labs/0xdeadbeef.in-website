Professional Statement

Cross-domain technical problem solving.

0xdeadbeef works across technical, creative, scientific, and strategic disciplines to design complete systems rather than isolated components.

Projects may involve architecture, research, source-code development, embedded hardware, firmware, automation, data systems, cryptography, Bitcoin infrastructure, artificial intelligence, security engineering, OSINT, investigations, documentation, visual design, and operational planning.

The preferred approach begins with the real operating environment: users, constraints, risks, dependencies, evidence, security boundaries, ownership, deployment, maintenance, and failure conditions.

Deliverables are designed to be understandable, inspectable, maintainable, versioned, documented, and appropriate to the legal and operational context in which they will be used.

Professional Value

Why engage 0xdeadbeef.

Multidisciplinary analysis

Technical problems are examined across software, hardware, security, economics, user behavior, operations, design, and long-term maintenance.

Independent judgment

Recommendations are based on technical reality, evidence, risk, and practical constraints rather than vendor incentives or institutional fashion.

Rapid prototyping

Concepts can be translated into working software, embedded prototypes, interfaces, diagrams, technical plans, or research tools quickly enough to test assumptions early.

Security-conscious architecture

Privacy, trust boundaries, authentication, key handling, failure recovery, logging, compartmentalization, and operational risk are considered from the beginning.

Documentation-centered delivery

Systems are accompanied by architecture notes, setup instructions, operating procedures, limitations, dependencies, testing records, and maintenance guidance.

Long-term systems thinking

Solutions are evaluated for maintainability, portability, ownership, cost, training, replacement, interoperability, resilience, and institutional continuity.

Professional Terms

Clear boundaries and written agreements.

All substantive work should be governed by written scope, payment terms, ownership, licensing, confidentiality, access, security requirements, deliverables, limitations, and dispute-resolution terms.

Consulting advice, project development, investigations, research, and contracting are separate engagement types and may require different agreements.

No public page constitutes an automatic offer, guaranteed availability, legal advice, financial advice, security warranty, employment agreement, partnership, or promise of results.

Bitcoin payment may be required for selected engagements. Payment terms, refunds, milestones, and acceptance conditions must be defined in the governing agreement.

Sensitive Work

Enhanced review for high-risk engagements.

Authorization required

Work involving offensive security, surveillance, critical infrastructure, private data, malware, restricted systems, law-enforcement support, national-security interests, digital evidence, or controlled technical information requires explicit written authorization and appropriate legal review.

Potential requirements may include identity verification, proof of authority, nondisclosure agreements, secure communication, compartmentalized access, evidence handling, audit trails, data-retention limits, disclosure procedures, and jurisdictional review.

Requests that are unlawful, deceptive, abusive, indiscriminate, exploitative, or incompatible with the stated ethical and defensive standards of the practice will not be accepted.

Availability

Selective engagement only.

Available selectively for consulting, contracting, technical review, architecture, Bitcoin systems, software, firmware, hardware, AI, cybersecurity, OSINT, investigations, documentation, research, and specialized engineering.

Priority is given to well-defined work with legitimate purpose, responsible stakeholders, realistic timelines, adequate funding, and clear authority.

Availability varies according to project complexity, security requirements, active research commitments, travel, client conflicts, and the degree of direct involvement required.

An initial inquiry should contain enough information to determine whether a serious qualification review is warranted.
